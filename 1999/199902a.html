Sunday 21 February 1999, 17:02

Is it OK if we just pretend February never happened? Write it off as a bad idea and start afresh (perhaps) in March?

You'll have to excuse my tardiness with respect to this journal-updating malarkey. Things have been more than a little busy, and somewhat strange around here over the last month. Sometimes I wonder if perhaps those who control my life have been reading this journal - I'm suddenly working much harder than previously, which is obviously great, but a total contrast to a couple of months ago. You can't help but think someone might've read my grumblings about being bored and decided to try teaching me a lesson!?

Similarly, there are some people who not only read the journal, but use it as an excuse to insult me, and attack my lifestyle! I read Diary-L for months before starting a journal, and knew that online journals had a tendency to generate some malicious comments from certain elements of society, but it's a weird and unpleasant feeling when it actually happens - makes me wonder if it's worth continuing the thing at all. My old paper-based diaries may be dull and unread by others, but at least nobody has ever insulted me for writing them. I briefly considered blocking access to my pages from certain IP addresses, but that seems petty and defeats the whole point of a truly open journal. So, it's still gonna be all or nothing around here, once I get back into the swing of things.

As well as working hard, I've also got a number of other projects on the boil just now - Bagger is coming along nicely, serving as a useful way of learning Visual Basic and Access. Shame the actual underlying database is taking an awful long time to populate, but it should be pretty funky when it's all finished. Meanwhile, I'm also trying to learn OPL, the Psion programming language, as I feel many of the shareware programs out there could be improved on (and also 'cos I loathe paying the UKP10 shareware fees for something I could do myself (eg an imperial to metric converter).

In other news.. the social scene is continuing nicely (I'm just getting over a hangover as I write this) - spent yesterday evening being lairy, obnoxious and more than a little bit drunk in Manchester, to celebrate Lexy's birthday. Started drinking at about 2:30pm, and didn't stop until I fell asleep in a club, which no doubt caused much amusement to the other patrons. Did me the world of good though! Two weeks time we're supposed to be doing something similar for my birthday (which kinda crept up without me noticing), but have yet to finalise the arrangements. And in a month, I'm off for a quick weekend in Scotland - yeah, I guess I'm hooked now; can't believe it's only 10 months since my first munro almost killed me and I vowed never to climb another as long as I lived. Hmmm...
